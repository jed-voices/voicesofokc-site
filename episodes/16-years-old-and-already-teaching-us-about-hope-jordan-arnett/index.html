Show notes

Some stories don't just inspire you.
They quietly change the way you see hope.

In this episode of Voices of OKC, the roles flip as 16-year-old City Center student Jordan Arnett steps into the host seat and interviews Jed about redemption, purpose, and the road that led to City Center. But the conversation quickly turns into something deeper.

Jordan shares his own story.
Growing up with instability.
A season of drifting in eighth grade.
And the moment that became a turning point—August 20, 2023.

With remarkable honesty and wisdom beyond his years, Jordan talks about faith, community, and what to do when life feels low. His reminder is simple but powerful:

"Happiness doesn't equal joy."

This is a conversation about resilience, redemption, and the reality that hope is still possible—no matter your past or your age.

What listeners will hear in this episode:

  • Why the hardest chapters of life can still become turning points

  • The difference between temporary happiness and lasting joy

  • How faith, community, and honest relationships shape resilience

  • Practical steps for navigating seasons of doubt, drift, and discouragement

  • A powerful reminder that your story is not finished

Listen now and discover why the next generation may be carrying more wisdom than we realize.
