USE WISELY: ADAM COURY ON LEADERSHIP, LEARNING, AND THE NEXT GENERATION

VOICES of OKC Wed, 15 Apr 2026 09:04:36 -0500 Show notes from Podbean
YouTube Apple Spotify
Show notes

Some conversations are really about more than the topic on the surface. This is one of them.

In this episode of Voices of OKC, Adam Coury joins the conversation to talk about AI, but what emerges is really a deeper reflection on discernment, responsibility, and leadership. As technology moves faster into daily life, work, and education, the question is no longer whether it matters. The question is whether people are willing to understand it before it begins shaping them.

Adam speaks honestly about the danger of avoidance. He argues that AI should be treated as a tool, not a voice to follow, and that the people who refuse to engage with it may leave the next generation to carry consequences they did not choose. This conversation matters because it pushes past fear and novelty and asks something more grounded: how do we remain fully human while learning to live wisely in a changing world?
